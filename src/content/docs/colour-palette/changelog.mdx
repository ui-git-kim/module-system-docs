### Refactor

- **Harmony pipeline rewritten as cusp-line projection.** The previous five-stage pipeline (H-K corrections, warm-lift, neon bell, multi-pass refinement) has been replaced by a single-pass geometric construction. `harmonyMap` decomposes the source colour as `(t, r)` inside a perceptual sub-triangle (vertices: `A` at the threshold-curve Ã— gamut-boundary intersection, `B` at the constant-chroma threshold Ã— gamut-boundary intersection, and `cusp` at the gamut peak). It then reconstructs the destination using the same `(t, r)` in the destination's sub-triangle. The mapping is mathematically self-inverse: `harmonyMap(harmonyMap(x, 180), 180) === x` in float OKLCH, with hex round-tripping limited only by 8-bit channel quantisation.
- **New helper `findThresholdCurveEdge(h, loL, hiL, rising)`.** Binary-searches for the L value where the gamut boundary crosses the chromatic-threshold *curve* (which itself varies with L). Used to place vertex A on the rising side where the gamut narrows fast.
- **Green/cyan neon suppression.** A Gaussian chroma cap centred at H=165Â° (Ïƒ=35Â°, peak strength 30%) pulls chroma back from the gamut boundary in the green-cyan band only, where sRGB's unusually wide gamut produced overwhelming neon results. Other hues are untouched.
- **Removed:** `CUSP_L_CEILING`, `warmBase` / `warmLiftL` / `warmLiftC`, `neonBell`, `neonRisk`, `coolnessSharp`, `hkDeficit`, the asymmetric Pass 5 reduction, and the dual-cusp pattern. The single-pass cusp-line projection subsumes all of these geometrically.
- **`harmonyMap` no longer calls `gamutMap3DShaded`.** The destination sits inside its own sub-triangle by construction, so containment is guaranteed without a final mapping step.
